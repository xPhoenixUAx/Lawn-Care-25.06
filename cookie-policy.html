Cookie Policy

Cookie and tracking information for this connection website.

This policy explains how cookies and similar technologies may relate to using a free lawn care provider connection service.

What cookies and similar technologies are

Cookies are small files that a website or related service may store through your browser. Similar technologies can include local storage, pixels, tags, scripts, server logs, device identifiers, and embedded third-party resources. These tools can help websites function, remember settings, measure use, secure forms, or support communications.

This Cookie Policy should be read together with the Privacy Policy and Terms & Conditions. Because this site operates as an aggregator-style connection service, cookies are separate from the information you voluntarily submit in a request form, phone call, text, or email.

Current website design

This website is designed to be lightweight. It does not require users to create accounts, log in, store carts, or use a payment portal on the site. The primary interaction is submitting or initiating a service request so homeowner details can be routed toward relevant independent providers.

At this time, the static website itself is not intended to set marketing cookies, advertising pixels, retargeting tags, Google Analytics IDs, Google Tag Manager IDs, Meta Pixel IDs, TikTok Pixel IDs, or ad conversion IDs unless those tools are later added and disclosed.

Strictly necessary and functional technologies

Some technical processes may be necessary for the website to load, display pages, process forms, prevent abuse, or keep the site secure. These may include server logs, hosting-level security tools, browser caching, or scripts required for basic site behavior such as mobile navigation, form handling, and interface effects.

Blocking all browser storage or scripts may affect how menus, forms, icons, fonts, or page layout behave, even if the website remains mostly readable.

Third-party resources

The website may load third-party resources such as Google Fonts or icon libraries. When your browser requests those resources, the third-party provider may receive technical information such as your IP address, browser details, and the page where the resource was requested. Those providers may handle data under their own policies.

If additional third-party tools are added in the future for analytics, call tracking, spam prevention, advertising, embedded maps, chat, or appointment tools, this Cookie Policy should be updated to describe those categories more clearly.

Analytics and performance cookies

Analytics cookies or similar tools, if added later, may help understand page visits, referral sources, device types, popular service categories, and form interaction patterns. The purpose would be to improve the website and make homeowner request intake clearer.

Analytics data should not be treated as a guarantee that a provider is available, qualified, licensed, insured, or suitable for a particular job. It would be used for website improvement, not provider certification.

Advertising and lead-related technologies

Advertising or lead attribution technologies, if added later, may help understand which campaigns or pages resulted in homeowner requests. If used, these tools may involve cookies, pixels, or tracking parameters. They may be subject to opt-out choices depending on the tool and applicable law.

The use of lead attribution would not change the aggregator model: the site would still be a free connection service, and independent providers would remain responsible for their own communications, estimates, services, and privacy practices.

How cookies relate to submitted request information

Cookies may identify a browser or session, but the more important information for provider matching is the information you choose to submit, such as contact details, address or service area, service category, property notes, timing, and photos. That submitted information is handled under the Privacy Policy.

Clearing cookies from your browser does not automatically delete a service request that you already submitted, and it does not automatically remove information already shared with independent providers.

Your browser controls

You can usually block, delete, or limit cookies through your browser settings. Browser controls vary by device and browser. You can also use private browsing modes or third-party privacy tools, though some website features may not behave exactly the same.

If future analytics or advertising tools are added, additional opt-out tools may be available through the relevant provider, browser settings, device settings, or industry opt-out mechanisms.

Do Not Track and global privacy signals

Some browsers send Do Not Track or global privacy signals. Because there is no single universally adopted standard for every signal, the website may not respond to all signals in the same way. Where required by applicable law and technically feasible, privacy preference signals may be honored according to the relevant legal requirement.

Updates to this policy

We may update this Cookie Policy if the website changes, if new tools are added, or if legal requirements change. The updated version will apply when posted unless a later effective date is stated.
