Sod repair

Patch bare areas and rebuild damaged turf with a realistic recovery plan.

We repair thin, worn, or damaged lawn areas with soil preparation, sod placement, watering guidance, and follow-up maintenance recommendations.

Repair work starts with the reason the lawn failed.

New sod can only succeed if the underlying problem is addressed. We look for compaction, shade, drainage, foot traffic, pet damage, irrigation gaps, grading issues, and mowing stress before recommending patch repair or larger replacement.

Service includes

  • Damaged turf removal and edge preparation
  • Soil loosening, minor leveling, and amendment recommendations
  • Fresh sod placement with tight seams and clean transitions
  • Initial watering guidance and early-care instructions
  • Follow-up mowing height and traffic recommendations
  • Identification of irrigation or drainage problems that could affect recovery

Good candidates for sod repair

Sod repair works well for bare patches near walkways, small pet-damaged zones, construction scars, heavy-use areas, erosion spots, and visible front-lawn damage where a faster result is needed than seeding alone can provide.

After installation

New sod needs consistent moisture, limited traffic, careful first mowing, and balanced maintenance. We can pair repair with irrigation support, fertilization timing, and recurring mowing so the new area blends into the existing lawn.
