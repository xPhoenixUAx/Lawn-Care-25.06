Irrigation support

Watering guidance that helps turf stay healthy without waste.

We inspect sprinkler coverage, dry zones, overspray, runoff, and schedule timing so lawn care and watering work together.

Watering is one of the biggest drivers of lawn health.

Too little water causes heat stress, thinning, and brown patches. Too much water encourages shallow roots, runoff, fungus pressure, and wasted resources. Our irrigation support focuses on practical adjustments that improve coverage and support the broader lawn care plan.

What we check

  • Sprinkler heads blocked by grass, shrubs, or shifted soil
  • Uneven spray patterns and missed dry areas
  • Overspray onto sidewalks, siding, fences, and driveways
  • Controller timing that does not match season or soil conditions
  • Runoff, pooling, and compacted areas that need a different approach
  • Watering recommendations after fertilization, sod, or repair work

When to schedule

Irrigation support is especially useful before summer heat, after landscape installation, after sod repair, when dry spots appear, or when water is visibly running off before the soil absorbs it.

Service limits

We provide lawn-focused irrigation review and minor adjustment guidance. Major plumbing repairs, backflow work, electrical controller replacement, and code-regulated irrigation work may require a licensed irrigation contractor.
